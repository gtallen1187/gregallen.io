Stuff I like

Below is a list of products and services that I use on a daily basis and highly recommend. Some of these products are free, others are not.

The links below are referral links. If you click one, and then immediately create an account for that product/service, then we both receive some type of reward.

FinTech

  • Robinhood - Fantastic app and completely free ($0 trading fees). Simple and fun way to get your feet wet trading stocks
    • Sign up using this link and we each get one free share of a randomly-picked stock. Not required to add funds to your account; literally a free stock just for signing up
  • Coinbase - Cryptocurrency wallet and online marketplace
    • Sign up using the link above and purchase $100 of any digital currency and we both receive $10 of free Bitcoin
  • Digit - Automatically save money without even noticing. Digit uses machine learning to study your bank account, then automatically transfers small amounts of money into a dedicated savings account at times when it knows you won't notice.
    • Sign up using the above link and we both receive a $5 bonus
  • Acorns - Similar to that old "Keep the change" program, except it puts the spare change into an investment account
  • Wealthfront - Automated Investment Services (robo-advisor)
    • Sign up using the above link and we both get an additional $5,000 managed for free.
  • Billshark - Upload your bills and their team negotiates a better rate on your behalf. They keep a portion of what you save
    • I saved $72 ($120 - 40% commission) simply by uploading a PDF of my Comcast bill
  • Simple - Easy online banking
  • Chime - Easy online banking
  • Amex Platinum Card - Hands down, best credit card out there
  • Google Pay - Like Apple Pay, but with a lot more features & capabilities
  • Venmo - Easiest way to quickly transfer money electronically

Mobile Phone

  • Project Fi - Best service on this list. Ditch the whole Verizon/AT&T/Sprint nonsense and make Google your wireless provider. Great coverage, no long-term contract, and super cheap
    • Manage your entire wireless service through a simple app
    • My cell phone bill is now ~$40 a month
    • Requires a Google phone (Nexus or Pixel)

If you're one of those people who have only had iPhones for the past decade, then you likely have an outdated opinion of Android. You should give the Google Pixel phone an honest look; Android isn't the same complicated, ugly, & bloated system that it used to be.

Self Improvement

  • Exist - Really cool. Aggregates all your personal data and identifies correlations between behavior patterns and mood. In other words, it helps you discover what activities & behaviors ultimately bring you the most happiness
  • RescueTime - Tracks time spent in various applications (computer & phone) to give you insights into what makes you productive versus what commonly distracts you. Works great with Exist
  • Fitbit - Activity tracker that you've probably heard of by now
  • New Vision Nutrition - Healthy pre-made meal plans delivered to your door
    • Unlike Blue Apron, all meals are pre-made and microwaveable. So you don't need to spend 45 minutes zesting a lemon

Tech Misc

  • Keybase - A social network, messaging system, and file sharing service, all based on cryptography
    • Great intro into the crypto world for those who know nothing about it (like me)
  • Heap - Best web analytics platform I've ever used
  • Digital Ocean - Simplified cloud hosting
  • Google Authenticator - Simple Two-Factor Authentication app that works with everything; replaces all those annoying text messages
    • Available for iPhone and Android
    • Side note: two-factor auth is important
  • Google Opinion Rewards - Quick 3-question surveys to earn free Google Play credits. Android only
    • I generate enough Google play credits through this app to earn ~1 free movie rental per week
  • Honey - Chrome extension that automatically applies discount codes on the checkout screen for major online retailers
  • Hired - Simple & effective recruiting platform
  • GitHub - Simplified code hosting & collaboration
  • Gusto - Easy-to-use payroll/benefits management system
  • Kite - Smart copilot for programmers
  • Tile - Handy bluetooth trackers to help you find stuff (keys, wallet, etc)

Travel

  • Uber - On-demand transportation
  • Lyft - Same as Uber
  • Fasten - Same as Lyft
  • Tripcase - Easy travel planning
    • You can forward any hotel/flight/train confirmation emails to trips@tripcase.com and it generates a useful, shareable itinerary for you
  • Rocketmiles - Generic travel booking site, except you get a ton of frequent flyer miles when you use it
  • National Car Rental - Simple and easy rental cars

Delivery

  • Google Express - On-demand delivery for groceries and a bunch of other stuff
  • Drizly - On-demand alcohol delivery
  • Instacart - On-demand grocery delivery
  • Rinse - Same as the fallen Washio; on-demand laundry delivery

  • Caviar - On-demand food delivery, including restaurants that don't normally deliver
  • goPuff - On-demand snack delivery

Software

No referral links here, this is simply a list of software programs that I find incredibly helpful and use on a daily basis.

These are all bolded because they're all awesome.

  • Bartender 3 - Cleans up your top menu bar by consolidating all app icons behind a single, expandable icon. Simple, but effective.
  • SizeUp - Easy-to-use keyboard-centric window manager
  • Atom - A great text editor, built by GitHub. Definitely try out the Beta version
  • Recordit - Simple utility to easily create videos or GIFs from screen recordings
  • DaisyDisk - Beautiful & powerful disk space analysis tool
  • Skitch - Tool to quickly create & annotate screenshots
  • iTerm2 - A powerful, user-friendly terminal replacement for Macs
  • HyperDock - Shows multi-window previews when hovering over application icons in the dock
  • Setapp - A platform containing a wide variety of installable Mac apps, with a single monthly subscription to access all of them. Some great/useful apps included in Setapp:
    • Just like any bundle, Setapp contains some great apps, and also some worthless ones. But for $8.99 per month, it's well worth it
      • Boxy - A desktop client for Inbox by Gmail
      • Flume - A great desktop client for Instagram
      • Bartender 3 - The first app listed above
      • BetterTouchTool - A tool for customizing the Macbook Pro Touch Bar
